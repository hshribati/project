Falafel

What is Falafel?

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern dish made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices. It's usually served in a pita or on a plate, accompanied by salads, pickled vegetables, hot sauce, and tahini-based sauces.

History of Falafel

Originating in the Middle East, falafel has been enjoyed for centuries. It is believed to have been invented in Egypt, and over time, it became a staple across the region, each country adding its unique twist to the dish.
